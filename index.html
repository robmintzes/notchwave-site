NotchWave

Live audio visualization in your MacBook's menu bar

NotchWave captures your system audio and renders real-time frequency visualizations in your menu bar, flanking the notch so the audio appears to emanate from it. The overlay is fully transparent to clicks — your menu bar stays completely accessible. Powered by CoreAudio and Metal.

  • Real-time frequency band visualization via CoreAudio process tap
  • Multiple visual modes — sparks, solid, fade — and nine segment shapes
  • Stereo mode — left dots track the left channel, right dots track the right
  • Multi-monitor — one window per screen, adapts on connect/disconnect
  • Adjustable sensitivity, color, bloom, grid density, and blend math
  • Lightweight menu-bar app — runs unobtrusively
  • Audio is never recorded or stored — processed in real time only

Installation note

This app is not signed with an official Apple Developer certificate. macOS may block it from opening. To fix this, open Terminal and run:

xattr -cr /path/to/NotchWave.app

Then open the app normally.

◉

Menu bar icon placement

On first launch, the NotchWave menu bar icon may appear behind other icons or buttons. To move it to a visible spot, open Control Center, click Edit Controls, then drag the NotchWave icon to your preferred position.
